Andrew Roche - 2967864

My name is Andrew Roche, I am a 28 year-old Computing Science student at Griffith College. I have had an interest in computers and computing technology ever since I was young, when my family first got a computer - an old Dell desktop computer which sat on the kitchen table for several years. As a kid I was always curious about how it worked, but mostly saw it just as something to play games on.

After finishing secondary school, I enrolled at Dublin City University to begin working on a degree in Biotechnology, as it was a field that I had grown to become quite interested in. However, by the time the first year had finished and I had gotten a look first-hand at what such a career entailed, I decided that this was no longer a field I wanted to work in and made the decision to leave the course and re-assess what I wanted to do in life.

Over the following years I found myself taking up jobs in the tech industry. My first job was as a tech-support agent for Microsoft, providing support for the Xbox 360 and the Windows Phone. After a a couple of years there I decided that I'd had enough of unhappy people complaining down a phone at me, and left. The following year or so I spent travelling around Europe, visiting a number of countries I had long had an interest in seeing such as France, Germany, Spain, Italy, Turkey and more.

After returning from my travels, I settled down and took up work with Sky, working again in the support sector providing support for their broadband customers. It wasn't long before I again grew tired of constant customer complaints and began thinking about a sizable change in careers. I had long had an interest in computers, in particular programming, but had never really acted upon that interest myself. With a growing desire for something new, I decided to leave my job at Sky and enroll in a Computing Science course, in the hope of finding a new career in a field more to my interests.
